## 8. Key Insights (Inverted Logic Perspective)

### 8.1 The "Special Departure" Fallacy
The government document labels the MAJORITY of services as "special" (`*`), when in fact:
- **Standard Service (`*`)**: 85-95% of all departures
- **Limited/Bypass Service (no mark)**: 5-15% of all departures (mainly early morning/late night)

### 8.2 Why This Labeling Exists
Likely regulatory reasons:
1. The "Limited/Bypass" service (called "normal" in the document) represents the "base" or "minimum" service level
2. The "Standard Service" (called "special" in the document) represents the "enhanced" service with additional stops
3. Government filings often define the simplest version as "standard" even if it's not the most used

### 8.3 Practical Impact
- **Passengers should expect** to board a Standard Service bus (stops at On Luk Street)
- **If you need On Luk Street**, 85-100% of buses will stop there depending on time/day
- **Limited/Bypass buses** are the exception - mainly early morning (before 7am) and late night (after 8:20-9:40pm)
- **Short-working services** are always Standard Service

### 8.4 Service Frequency Reality

|--------|-------|
| **Peak morning frequency** | Every 10-20 minutes (Standard Service) |
| **Peak evening frequency** | Every 15-20 minutes (Standard Service) |
| **Off-peak frequency** | Every 20-30 minutes (Standard Service) |
